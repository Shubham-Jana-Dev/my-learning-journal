### ğŸ“ DAA Deep Dive: Big O Notation Formalism
Refined the mathematical understanding of asymptotic analysis.

**Core Definition:**
Assuming $f(n)$ and $g(n)$ are non-negative functions:
$$f(n) = O(g(n)) \iff f(n) \leq c \cdot g(n) \text{ for all } n \geq n_0$$
*Where $c$ and $n_0$ are constants.*

**Key Takeaways:**
* **Tight Upper Bound:** $g(n)$ represents the worst-case growth rate for $f(n)$.
* **Asymptotic Growth:** After a specific point $n_0$, the function $g(n)$ scaled by $c$ will always stay above $f(n)$.
